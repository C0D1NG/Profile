  • Behavioral finance Quantitative trading

    Financial Markets

    I was introduced to financial markets at the age of 8 by some weird names and images of charts in the newspaper that made no sense. I was astounded when people told me that the stock market was a gamble and I should probably try to stay away from it.

    Fast forward to the age of 16, I was a young fresher who started pursuing a computer science degree. My university(LPU) had been the most diverse place I have been to in terms of cultures, people and languages. Computers have been the only thing I aspired to work for. Still, this tiny spark for financial markets was turned to blazing fire when I came to know that I had a choice to choose Finance as a Side-Major.

    Without skipping a beat, I signed up for the major in financial markets, which introduced me to a new spectrum of possibilities. I saw a different world, from commodity markets to the latest crypto markets; it was fascinating to understand the complex financial systems that run the world.

    I started learning Behavioural Finance to understand the influence of psychology on the behaviour of human beings in their financial decision-making. It helps in identifying dozens of financial decision-making errors that can be avoided.

    With the newly acquired knowledge, I started trading initially by manually placing orders on different trading platforms. But as my programming knowledge grew, I began designing systems to analyse charts. I have also been investing in crypto and NFT markets.

    Nowadays, I work on designing systems for quantitative trading by exploring different avenues of machine learning and learning Behavioural Finance.
